Lets start off with some of my TOP 10 Albums (in a list element, no less...)

  1. The Division Bell - Pink Floyd

    I can't believe you're goint to read all this. Despite it being their last album (until The Endless River came out in 2014),
    this was actually the first Pink Floyd album I heard. Being a wee sprog I hadn't been exposed to psychdelic rock before,
    and withough waxing lyrical, it absolutely captivated me.

    As I grew older and got my first Ipod (which was so thick you could use it as a purging weapon), I started listening to
    more of their classic albums such as The Dark Side of the Moon and The Wall , but I always found myself leaning
    towards the later Gilmour era of Pink Floyd - I guess I just prefered his bassy, calming tone? Don't get me wrong, I love
    schreeching "WE DON'T NEED NO EDUCATION" in my naff Southern accent as much as the next guy, but The Division Bell
    will always be the definitive Pink Floyd sound to me...

  2. Brothers in Arms - Dire Straights

    Continuing on with the Dad rock, we have Dire Straight's definitive album Brothers In Arms. This record is so nostalgic,
    taking me back to hearing it's title track for the first time during a holiday I was lucky enough to be on with the family.

    This album has everything. Need a dose of optimism after a break-up? Try Why Worry? Fancy spicing things up a little with
    some sweet sweet saxaphone? Your Latest Trick is the one for you. Wanna impress your friends by singing WOO-HOO
    at the wrong point in the song every. single. time? Then Walk of Life has you covered.

  3. Out Of The Blue - Electric Light Orchestra

    Maybe Top 10 is a little too far. This is a Stockholm syndrom kind of love. If I had a pound for every time this was played
    on repeat in the confines of my Dad's car... admittedly I'd only have about Â£16. But rest assured, it was enough to hard wire
    every single lyric into my head like some kind of experimental 70's mind control.

    I gotta admit it though, this album is banger after banger. Hearing that deep synth creep in from a fade as the drums escalate
    at the start of Turn to Stone gets me absolutely P U M P E D every time! Mr Blue Sky is an bona fide classic that everyone
    can tap their foot to, the strings in Sweet Talkin' Woman are just 👌👌👌mwa magnifique; and let's be honest, if by time the
    album rolls to a close you find that you HAVEN'T cried at Wild West Hero , well... then you're probably dead inside...
